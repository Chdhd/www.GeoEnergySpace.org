Foundation

Built on the belief that data should reach the ground.

GESNRI — the GeoEnergy Space Nexus Research Institute — was founded on a simple observation: the tools that can see a drought forming, a flood rising, or a grid failing already exist, but the people who need that information most rarely have access to it in a usable form.

We work as a bridge between satellite-scale science and ground-level decision-making. Our researchers combine Earth observation, geospatial modelling, and energy systems analysis to produce open datasets, tools, and briefings that universities, NGOs, government agencies, and communities can act on directly.

Headquartered in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, GESNRI is positioned at the convergence of a region shaped by both climate vulnerability and rapid growth in space and geospatial capacity — giving our work an immediacy that purely academic research often lacks.

How we're organized

Three pillars, one nexus.

Every project GESNRI undertakes sits within one of three interlocking pillars — each a distinct orbit around the same core mission of translating data into impact.

PILLAR I

Space & Spatial Data

Earth observation, satellite imagery analysis, and geospatial data infrastructure that make remote-sensing science usable for planning and response.

PILLAR II

Humanitarian, Climate & Energy

Applied research connecting climate intelligence and sustainable energy systems to humanitarian outcomes — from disaster resilience to rural electrification.

PILLAR III

Space Exploration

Forward-looking research and education in space science and exploration, building regional capacity and curiosity for the next generation.

What we do

Functionality across the research lifecycle.

Open data platforms

We build and maintain public datasets and dashboards so partners can query, download, and build on our findings directly, rather than waiting on a report.

Applied research & modelling

From flood-risk mapping to energy-load forecasting, our research is designed to answer a specific operational question, not just publish a finding.

Partnerships & convening

We work alongside universities, NGOs, government agencies, and private-sector partners to co-design projects that fit real institutional needs.

Capacity building

Workshops, fellowships, and training programs that grow local capability in geospatial science, energy systems analysis, and space research.
